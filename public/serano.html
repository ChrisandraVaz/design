Overview

Transforming an outdated digital presence into an engaging experience that authentically represents the bakery's warmth.

As the lead designer for Serano Cafe's website redesign, I transformed their outdated digital presence into an engaging, user-friendly experience that authentically represents their brand. Working closely with the cafe owners, I reimagined their site architecture, developed a cohesive visual language, and created intuitive pathways for visitors.

What's Serano Cafe? Serano Cafe is a cozy, artisanal Greek bakery known for its handcrafted pastries and warm atmosphere. The website redesign aimed to encapsulate the bakery's inviting essence, improve user experience, and modernize the branding.

Background

The Challenge

Serano Cafe's original website did not reflect the warm, inviting nature of the bakery. The outdated design lacked cohesive branding, had a cluttered navigation system, and did not effectively showcase the bakery's artisanal products.

My goal was to create a visually appealing, user-friendly website that enhances the online presence and improves customer engagement.

Business Goals

Through initial conversations with the bakery owners and analyzing customer feedback, we established several key objectives:

Capture the Atmosphere

Create a digital experience that authentically captures the bakery's warm, inviting atmosphere

Intuitive Navigation

Develop an intuitive navigation system that helps visitors find information effortlessly

Showcase Products

Showcase their artisanal products with high-quality imagery and compelling descriptions

Emotional Connection

Strengthen the emotional connection with customers through storytelling elements

Motivation

Why Not Just Use a Template?

In theory, a template-based website builder could have provided a quick, inexpensive solution for Serano Cafe. But would a cookie-cutter approach truly capture their unique character and craftsmanship?

For a bakery that prides itself on handcrafted, made-from-scratch products, a generic template would contradict their core values. Their digital presence needed to reflect the same attention to detail and quality that went into their pastries.

Just as Serano Cafe carefully selects ingredients and perfects their recipes, we needed to thoughtfully design every element of their digital experience to create something truly special.

Challenges

Balancing Aesthetics and Functionality

As I began translating our structural plans into visual designs, I encountered several challenges that required thoughtful solutions:

Creating an Intuitive Navigation System

The original site had a confusing navigation structure. I tested several navigation patterns with users and ultimately settled on a streamlined main menu with clear labels, persistent access to hours and location, and contextual sub-navigation.

Showcasing Products Authentically

How could we represent the sensory experience digitally? I developed a strategy combining high-quality photography capturing texture and detail, descriptive language evoking flavor and aroma, and testimonials highlighting customer experiences.

Illustrative Accents

To enhance the bakery's digital presence, I incorporated hand-drawn vector elements in light blue to add character and accessibility icons. Initially explored 3D modeling in Blender, but transitioned to Illustrator for greater flexibility.

Takeaways

What I Learned

Visual Design

Through this project, I honed my skills in visual design. I learned that balancing aesthetics with functionality is crucial in web design.

Project Management & Sprint Planning

This project provided valuable experience in project management, particularly in sprint planning. Breaking down the project into manageable tasks helped me stay organized and meet goals.

User Feedback

Incorporating user feedback was essential for refining the designs and ensuring they resonated with the target audience. Designing for both digital and physical assets ensures brand consistency across all touchpoints.

Iterative Design Process

Through the iterative design process, I learned the importance of continuously refining designs based on feedback and insights. This approach allowed me to deliver high-quality deliverables.

What I'd Do Differently

If I were to approach this project again, I would:

  • Conduct user interviews earlier in the process to gather more direct insights
  • Create a more detailed content strategy document to guide future updates
  • Develop a more comprehensive analytics plan to track specific user behaviors
  • Include A/B testing of key conversion elements to optimize performance
